**Question:** What's the best security camera for a remote property with no WiFi or electricity?

**Answer:**

I run a small security company in Qatar and this exact question comes up all the time. Villas under construction, farms out in Al Khor, labour camps, even some villas in Lusail where the internet isn't connected yet.

The short answer: a solar-powered 4G camera.

No WiFi needed, no electrical wiring. The solar panel charges the battery during the day (plenty of sun here 350 days a year) and the camera connects through a regular SIM card, same as your phone.

We've been using a few different models for clients and here's what I've learned:

**If you just need to check in occasionally** — standard 2K solar cam with a decent size battery. Around 400-500 QAR. Put it up, point it where you need, check the app when you want. Works fine for monitoring a construction site or a farm.

**If you need active monitoring** — go for the PTZ version. You can pan and tilt remotely to follow movement. Costs more (around 900 QAR) but saves you installing 3-4 fixed cameras.

**What to watch out for:**
- Make sure it has a big enough solar panel. Some cheap ones barely trickle charge. In Qatar's summer that's fine, but you want enough buffer for the cloudy winter days.
- Check if the app lets you set motion zones. You don't want notifications every time a cat walks past.
- Get one with a physical SIM slot, not eSIM. Swapping carriers is easier if you need better coverage in a specific area.

Battery life varies. On a full charge with moderate motion recording, expect 5-7 days without any sun. With daily sun, it'll run indefinitely.

We've had units running for 8+ months now without issues — through last year's heat, dust storms, the works. The main thing is getting the panel angled properly. Too flat and dust settles on it, too steep and you lose midday efficiency.

If anyone's looking at these in Qatar specifically, Secuview has a few models I've tested that hold up well. Happy to answer specific questions.

Good luck with your setup.
